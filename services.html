System Integration

System Integration is the process of linking together different computing systems and software applications physically or functionally, to act as a whole. In today’s connected world, the role of system integration engineers is becoming more and more important: more and more systems are designed to connect, both within the system under construction and to systems that are already deployed. There are various integration mechanisms like Vertical Integration, Star Integration, Horizontal Integration or Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) and Common Data Format.

At Pace IT, our processes and several years of service experience are reflected in the success we have achieved in executing projects. Strategic alliances with leading technology players further enable us to deliver cost-effective integration services that are scalable, cloud-based, and innovative and result-oriented for your business. we deliver the cutting edge solutions to customers with our vendor agnostic approach.

Big Data

Every two years, the volume of data in the world doubles, and by 2015, it is estimated that this volume of data will reach 7.9 Zettabytes. Unstructured data, such as text and images account for 90% of this amount. In the future, it is highly anticipated that this massive amount of data i.e. Big Data, will be used in business analytics to improve operations and to offer innovative services.

Big Data is very wide in scope and its usage covers a large spectrum of applications from targeted advertisement and recommendation systems, to energy consumption estimation and smart metering.

With a wealth of experience in analytics, and a commitment to openness in developing IT infrastructure and tools, along with strong global BI deployment capabilities, PACE IT can help you unlock the business value from your Big Data.

Mobile App Development

Business Intelligence

We recognise that successful business intelligence (BI) can only be achieved through collaboration between the business and its technology suppliers.

We believe that a traditional waterfall approach, a sequential design process, is not best suited to BI development. Users need to see, query and report on the data in order to approve requirements and validate design.

Our developing BI requirements service enables us to work with our clients to iteratively develop prototypes quickly and then build a robust and lasting set of requirements that all parties understand and approve of.

The prototypes are developed within a controlled environment for a set of key users (the 'model office'). This prototyping provides the flexibility to develop new and emerging requirements in short succession allowing users to understand how these will work in practice.

Once all the requirements are identified and proven in the prototyping stage, we move to a more traditional sequential development lifecycle.
